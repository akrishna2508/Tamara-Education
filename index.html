Hackathon Basics

What is the Tamara National Hackathon? ▼
The Tamara National Hackathon is a national-level innovation competition for high school students (Grades 6–12) organized by Tamara Education. It challenges students to identify real-world problems and develop innovative solutions using science, technology, or research. Top performers gain pathways to IRIS National Fair and ISEF.
Who can participate? ▼
High school students from Grades 6–12 can participate, both individually or in teams of up to 3 members. The hackathon is open to students from India and internationally. Beginners are warmly welcomed — no prior research or competition experience is needed.
Is the hackathon online or offline? ▼
The initial idea submission and evaluation phases are conducted online, making it accessible to students across India and internationally. Selected shortlisted teams will receive further details about subsequent phases, which may include in-person or virtual mentorship sessions.
What are the team size and rules? ▼
Teams can consist of 1 to 3 members. All team members must be enrolled in grades 6–12. Each team can only submit one project idea. Teams must ensure their idea is original and has not been previously submitted to or won other competitions in the same form.

Registration & Payment

How do I register for the hackathon? ▼
Registration is done through the official Tamara Education website (tamaraedu.com). Students fill in the registration form with team details, school information, and project idea overview. After submission, you will receive a confirmation email with further instructions.
Is there a registration fee? ▼
Please contact Tamara Education directly via [email protected] or [email protected] or WhatsApp +91 8105488875 for the most current fee structure and registration details for the next hackathon cycle.
Is the registration fee refundable? ▼
Refund policies vary by hackathon cycle. Please refer to the terms and conditions provided at the time of registration or contact our team for clarification before making any payment.

Ideas & Submission

What types of ideas are allowed? ▼
Ideas can span any science or technology domain: AI/ML, Robotics, IoT, Healthcare Innovation, Environmental Science, Social Impact, and more. The key requirement is that the idea addresses a real-world problem with an innovative, research-backed solution. Ideas must be original and the student's own work.
What is the idea submission process? ▼
After registering, teams submit a structured idea document outlining: the problem being addressed, proposed solution, methodology, expected impact, and required resources. The submission window for the current cycle was open from January 15 to March 15, 2026.
Can we change our idea after submission? ▼
Minor refinements to your idea may be allowed during the mentorship phase. However, the core problem statement and solution approach should remain consistent with the original submission. Significant changes must be approved by your assigned mentor.
What are the evaluation criteria? ▼
Projects are evaluated on: Innovation and originality, Scientific methodology, Real-world applicability and impact, Quality of research and documentation, Feasibility of implementation, and Presentation quality. Evaluators include our panel of expert mentors and invited judges.

Certificates & Rewards

Do all participants receive certificates? ▼
Yes! Every participant who submits a valid project idea receives a Participation Certificate from Tamara Education. Shortlisted teams receive additional recognition, and winners receive merit certificates signed by our expert panel.
What prizes do winners receive? ▼
1st Prize: Complete project sponsorship by Tamara Foundation (research design, hardware, documentation, end-to-end guidance). 2nd Prize: Advanced mentorship with structured research methodology and competition preparation. 3rd Prize: Research mentorship to help transform your idea into a publishable or competition-ready project.
Are there internship opportunities? ▼
Outstanding performers may be considered for internship and collaboration opportunities with Tamara Education and our partner organizations. Selected teams may also receive long-term guidance toward ISEF and global science fairs.

Mentorship & Support

When will I get a mentor? ▼
The mentorship phase begins on April 15, 2026 for shortlisted teams. Each shortlisted team will be paired with a relevant mentor based on their project domain — AI/ML projects with Sonal Parsai, healthcare projects with Dr. Sveta Agarwal, and research projects with Krishna Lal Agarwal.
What does the mentorship involve? ▼
Mentorship includes: Regular check-in sessions, guidance on research design and methodology, feedback on prototypes and documentation, competition preparation coaching, and support for technical implementation. The frequency and format of sessions are agreed upon with your mentor.
What communication channels are available? ▼
You can reach Tamara Education via: WhatsApp at +91 8105488875, Email at [email protected] or [email protected], and through our social media channels on Instagram (@tamaraed_u) and Twitter (@tamara_edu).

General Queries

Can absolute beginners join? ▼
Absolutely! Tamara Education is designed for students at all experience levels. Our mentors will guide you through every step — from idea conception to final presentation. Many of our most successful alumni had no prior competition experience when they started.
Is college/school permission required? ▼
While formal school permission is not typically required for online participation, we strongly encourage students to inform their school/college about their participation. For any in-person events or to use school facilities for your project, you will need appropriate institutional approval.
What is the code of conduct? ▼
All participants must maintain academic integrity, submit original work, treat fellow participants and mentors with respect, and follow all communication guidelines. Any form of plagiarism, misconduct, or violation of these principles may result in disqualification.
What leads to disqualification? ▼
Participants may be disqualified for: submitting plagiarized or previously winning work, misrepresentation of team members or affiliations, violating the code of conduct, failure to meet submission deadlines without prior communication, or any form of unfair practice.
